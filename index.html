Introduction

All living organisms require food for energy, growth, repair, and survival. Plants are special organisms because they can make their own food using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide through the process called photosynthesis. In contrast, animals and humans rely on plants either directly or indirectly for their food.

Modes of Nutrition

Nutrition is the process by which organisms take in food and utilize it. In plants, we find two main modes:

  • Autotrophic Nutrition: Green plants make their own food using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide. They are called autotrophs.
  • Heterotrophic Nutrition: Some plants cannot make their own food and depend on other plants or animals for it. These are heterotrophs.

Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants synthesize their own food using chlorophyll, sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water.

Synthesis of Plant Food

Photosynthesis makes glucose, a simple sugar. Plants use glucose to make other important substances like:

  • Carbohydrates (like starch, for energy storage)
  • Proteins (requires nitrogen from the soil)
  • Fats (for energy storage, especially in seeds)

Other Modes of Nutrition in Plants

  • Parasitic Plants: Like Cuscuta (Amarbel), they absorb food from a host plant.
  • Insectivorous Plants: Like the Pitcher plant, they trap insects and digest them for nutrients.
  • Symbiotic Relationships: Like Lichens, where algae and fungi live together and help each other.

Saprotrophs and Fungi

Saprotrophs obtain nutrients from dead and decaying matter. They secrete digestive juices and absorb the decomposed nutrients.

Examples: Mushrooms, bread molds, and other fungi.

How Soil Gets Its Nutrients Back

  • Farmers use manures and fertilizers to restore nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.
  • Rhizobium bacteria in the roots of legumes naturally fix nitrogen from the air into the soil.

Glossary

  • Autotroph: An organism that makes its own food.
  • Heterotroph: An organism that gets its food from others.
  • Photosynthesis: The process by which plants prepare their food using sunlight.
  • Chlorophyll: The green pigment in leaves necessary for photosynthesis.
  • Parasite: An organism that lives on or in a host and gets its food from it.
  • Symbiosis: A close and long-term biological interaction between two different biological organisms.
