Autotrophic Plants

Autotrophic plants make their own food using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide in a process called photosynthesis. They make their own food by taking in Carbon Dioxide, Water and Sunlight to make Glucose (Did you know? When plants create Glucose during the process of photosynthesis, it immediately gets converted into Starch!) and exert Oxygen as a byproduct. Examples include trees, grasses, and most plants!

Formula of Autotrophic Plants

CO^2 (Carbon Dioxide) + H^2O (Water) + Light Energy (Gotten from the Sun) → C^2H^12O^6 (Glucose) + O^2 (Oxygen)

Heterotrophic Plants

Heterotrophic Plants are plants that use other plants to obtain water and nutrients for their growth and kill the other plants.

Rafflesia: The Largest Flower

The Rafflesia Arnoldii is known as the largest flower in the world, reaching up to 3 feet across! It is a parasitic plant that survives by stealing nutrients from other plants.

Herbarium

A herbarium is a collection of preserved plant specimens. In the process, plants are bleached and pressed flat to create beautiful transparent plant skeletons!
